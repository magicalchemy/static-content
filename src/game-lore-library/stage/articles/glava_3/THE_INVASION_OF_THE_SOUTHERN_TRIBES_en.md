# CHAPTER THREE. THE INVASION OF THE SOUTHERN TRIBES.


The visions and the map of the Ancients led Galdan to the Hayagta Plains in the southeast of the Dudzhun Empire. One of the ancient temples of Mendelef was to be found in these vast fields, where countless flocks of sheep grazed, and there were no major cities for many hundreds of miles, only small villages of steppe herders and lonely camps of shepherds. 

Galdan and his squad of Guardians buhlag had been wandering these plains for two weeks. The Council of Elders, knowing the burden the Chosen One carried on his shoulders, thought it prudent not to send the only member of the First Generation alone on the dangerous quest for the ancient temples. The safety of his life was the primary concern of all who accompanied him.

Unsurprisingly, the guards who accompanied Galdan were the most experienced and skillful warriors, for they had been carefully chosen from all the goblin tribes. It was rumored that some of the guards had even fought on the side of one of the mercenary teams of the Union of Invia, which said a lot about their fighting skills and experience.

One day, scouts reported that a large train of wagons and foot people was moving ahead of their troop. The goblins assumed it was one of the Dudzhun Empire's trade caravans on their way to Eastern Balkhia. 

But as the line of wagons and people approached where the goblins were hiding, it became clear that they were not traders but refugees.
    
The look of the people showed that they had traveled in great haste. The loads in the wagons were piled haphazardly as if they had been thrown in without care or attention to their safety. The clothes of many were torn and sooty.

All the people were exhausted from the long journey and the scorching summer heat, from which it was impossible to find shelter in these steppes. However, it was not fatigue on their faces but anxiety and fear. 

Galdan decided to go out to the wagon and find out what had happened to the refugees. The Chief Guardian, a gray-haired warrior of the Lonely Moon tribe, was against it, but the alchemist had already moved out of the thick grass toward the first group of men. The guards had no choice but to jump out after him quickly.

There were loud female screams. Some rushed away from the goblins who had appeared before them. Others fell to their knees and begged for mercy.  

The main stream of refugees stretching behind the first group stopped, not realizing what was happening ahead.  

The goblins did not expect such a reaction and stood at a loss for the first time, not daring to do anything. It took a long time to calm all the people and convince them that the goblins were not a threat.

Only then did the refugees tell the goblins about the terrible events that forced them to leave their homes and go on this challenging journey....
