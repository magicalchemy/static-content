# CHAPTER FIVE. THE HARBINGER OF THE STORM.

It had been a long day. A day filled with worries, burdens, and endless thoughts—so much so that it felt like it would never end. After supper with the others, Galdan could hardly wait to return to his room and collapse onto his bed. As he walked across the wooden floor, which creaked faintly beneath his steps, the goblin’s mind was occupied with everything happening in the world.

Tens of thousands of orcs had fled from the continent of Jharak, escaping the undead hordes. The undead. Even to Galdan, it sounded like madness—and he had personally interrogated an orc who had survived an attack by the legions of the dead. He was certain the orc hadn’t been lying; after all, he had prepared the truth serum himself.

The dim glow of wall-mounted torches flickered along the dark corridors. As he reached his door, a strange sensation crawled up his spine—his skin prickled as if charged with static.

Something was wrong.

Galdan tensed. He had long since learned to trust his instincts, the same instincts that had led him to the ancient temples of Mendalef time and time again. Taking a steadying breath and bracing himself for whatever awaited inside, he pushed open the door and stepped in.

Darkness greeted him, along with the familiar scents of alchemical ingredients. And rosemary. And mint. He often burned them to stave off the sleepless nights that plagued him.

Galdan didn’t head straight for his bed. Instead, he stood still, scanning the room, searching for what had set his nerves on edge. Then, he noticed it.

The window was open.

He could have sworn he had shut it before leaving that morning.

“What the…?” he muttered, stepping carefully toward it.

His left hand had already found the hilt of the dagger at his belt.

"I hope dinner was satisfying, Chosen One."

A low, measured voice drifted from the shadows, from the corner where an armchair stood.

Galdan spun, his blade flashing in the moonlight.

"I assure you, there’s no need for that. I mean you no harm."

A figure rose from the chair—short, old, and completely bald. In the dim glow of lamplight and moonbeams, Galdan could finally make out his uninvited guest. A goblin. His skin was pale, almost translucent, his face etched with deep wrinkles. His eyes were entirely white, clouded like thick mist. He was clad in flowing gray and white robes, and in his hands, he held a staff made of whitewood.

Looking at him, Galdan couldn’t shake the feeling that he had seen this face before.

But where?

"I have come to you in dreams, my boy," the old goblin said with a knowing smile.

"Who are you?" Galdan asked, forcing his voice to remain steady.

The elder furrowed his brow, as if pondering how best to answer.

"I have had many names. In different eras, I was called different things. The elves of the Golden Age knew me as the Gray Pilgrim... and they were far more hospitable than their present kin, I must say. But the past is the past—never forgotten, only carefully kept. Now, where was I…? Ah, yes."

The old goblin turned his sightless gaze upon Galdan, and a strange tingling sensation spread through the young alchemist’s body.

"You may call me Larus. I have come to help you—and this world—prevent a terrible catastrophe. Jida stands at the edge, and if nothing is done, it will fall into the abyss. You, my boy, have a most crucial role to play in the battle ahead."

A lump formed in Galdan’s throat.

"But… I may be a skilled alchemist… I may even be this ‘Chosen One’ that people speak of—but I am only one man. What can a lone alchemist do?"
Larus chuckled.

"Ah, a Chosen One with humility! Now that is a rare sight. But listen well, Galdan—I have already stood before the great and powerful of this world. I have traveled thousands of miles to warn them of the coming storm..."

The old goblin trailed off, his expression darkening with a weary sigh.

Galdan hesitated before breaking the silence.

"And?"

Larus lowered himself back into the chair.

"Listen carefully, and I will tell you."
