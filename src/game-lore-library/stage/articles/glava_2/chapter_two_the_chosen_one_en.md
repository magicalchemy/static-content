# CHAPTER TWO. THE CHOSEN ONE.
Galdan had not been an ordinary goblin for a single day of his fifteen years. Outwardly, he didn't differ much from his peers: he wasn't six feet tall, he didn't have three pairs of arms or legs, and he didn't shoot lightning from his eyes. It was all about his origin.

Fifteen years ago, a squad of the Seekers of Truth, led by the legendary Gorl, discovered a passageway into the hidden valley of Ehoron, which became the new home for the entire goblin race. Found there as well was the temple of the god Mendelef, the patron of goblins who had gifted them the art of alchemy. The temple harbored many secrets, and the most astonishing of them were the flasks containing the seeds of life. Many of them were cracked or completely shattered, but one remained perfectly intact, despite being covered in a thick layer of dust. Inside that very flask was a baby boy, whom Gorl named Galdan and took in to raise as his own.

Galdan was a natural-born alchemist. He brewed potions much faster than his peers, and their effects were far more potent. Galdan intuitively selected ingredients, sometimes without even knowing the recipes. The art of alchemy was in his very blood.

The youth had only two close friends: Balta, a blacksmith's apprentice who was as sturdy and straight as an arrow, and Delb, a nimble, fleet-footed goblin from the Gatherers' buleg. Together they grew up, got into trouble, found their way out of it, and overall lived the typical life of teenage goblins.

Sitting on a wooden bench beneath a cherry tree, Galdan tiredly rubbed the bridge of his nose. Lounging beside him, Balta was finishing off a large bun filled with jam. Delb, for what felt like the hundredth time, was studying scholar Dorg's "Herbology: How to Tell a Useful Weed from a Useless One." A sudden gust of wind kicked up a swirl of fine sand, causing Balta to sneeze violently right onto the open pages of the book.

"May the gout strike you, Balta!" Delb hissed, jerking away to the edge of the bench. He desperately tried to rub his companion's nose-dwellings off the "Common Oregano" entry, but only succeeded in smudging the ink.

"Try a handkerchief," the goblin replied, pulling a soiled rag of an indeterminate color from somewhere inside his tunic.

"Oh, forget you and your handkerchief! How am I supposed to return this book to the library now?"

"Take Galdan with you. Old Osh likes him," Balta reasoned casually, tossing the last piece of the bun into his mouth.

Darkness had completely settled over the street as the trio made their way toward the ancient library building, which was still undergoing repairs and expansions. Its lower floors stored thousands of books, manuscripts, and scrolls, some of which were so old they would literally crumble into dust at the slightest touch.

Delb's disgruntled muttering echoed softly through the musty, torch-lit room that smelled of mold and rotting paper. The goblins stopped before a massive writing desk piled high with parchment, empty inkwells, and heavy volumes. Seated behind the desk was old Osh, the scholar in charge of the place, who practically jumped out of his skin upon seeing the goblins.

"What are you doing here?!" Osh’s short-sighted eyes glared fiercely at the trio, a sharp note of hysteria piercing his voice.

The young goblins exchanged glances.

"We wanted to return a book," Galdan said hesitantly, holding out the Herbology volume to the librarian. The old man snatched it from the goblin's hands without even glancing at the title, shoved it into a desk drawer, and snapped, "Now shoo, go home! Or do you think I have nothing better to do than entertain you with idle chatter?"

Balta shrugged and was the first to head back, with the others scurrying right behind him. Once outside, the blacksmith's apprentice yawned, stretched thoroughly, and pulled an apple from his pocket.

"See? Nothing terrible happened. And you guys were worried!"

"I don't know, it seemed to me like Osh was troubled by something," Galdan shrugged. "He always checks the books thoroughly."

"Well, I guess he decided not to check them today then," Balta said, crunching into his apple.

"I'm just glad I made it out of there alive," Delb sighed with relief. "But you..." he jabbed a green finger toward Balta, "you..."

Galdan walked behind his companions, lost in his own thoughts. Later, left alone in his bedroom, tossing and turning on his hard, uncomfortable mattress, he couldn't close his eyes for a long time. He could feel it coming—something that would change his life in Dji`Da forever.
