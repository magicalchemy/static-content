# CHAPTER FOUR. AN ANCIENT EVIL.

While searching for the next place where the ruins of Mendelef's temple could be located, goblin squad met a mounted troop of the second imperial corps of the Dudjun army. This corps had been hastily withdrawn from the empire's northern borders and sent south to repel an invasion of orcs from across the sea. As soon as it became clear that there was an alchemist among the goblins, he was asked to arrive at the corps' field camp with all possible urgency. 

Galdan reached the camp and was immediately escorted to the main headquarters of the corps. There he was waited for by high-ranking officers headed by General Li. He told the alchemist that the front line of the division had encountered a small group of orcs, and at the cost of the death of dozens of soldiers, they had managed to capture an orc.  

The best interrogators had been working on the orc for three days, but he had not uttered a word, even under the most subtle torture. And the army - no, the Emperor himself - needed to know at least some information about the orcs. Who they were, where they came from, and their goals. It was a task of national importance. 

Therefore, upon hearing that goblins had been spotted near the camp, the general ordered them to be found and, if there was an alchemist among them, to bring him to the headquarters immediately. General Li knew that with the art of alchemy, a truth serum could be prepared that could loosen the tongue of any, even an intractable orc. In return, the general was willing to provide any reward the alchemist requested.   

Galdan agreed to help, and his only condition was that he be allowed to personally attend the orc's interrogation and hear what he had to say. General Lee had no choice but to agree to the demand. 

The preparation of the truth serum took all day. It wasn't until late in the evening, when it was ready, that the alchemist was led into a large tent surrounded by a double ring of guards. General Li, his personal guard, and a few other officers were already waiting for him inside the tent. All eyes of the gathered people were directed toward the center of the tent.   
There, chained to two pillars, stood an orc. Four Imperial soldiers stood around him with their swords at the ready.

The orc's huge, massive figure was awe-inspiring to anyone who saw him for the first time. The grown man was barely taller than the orc's chest, and his torso was twice as broad. It was clear why such precautions were necessary. 

The soldiers pulled on the chains that chained the orc, forcing him to his knees. Looking at the orc, Galdan noted that his body bore not only the scars of old wounds but also the fresh marks and burns left by all the various torture instruments of the imperial interrogators. He could only marvel at the orc's tremendous willpower and fortitude, for he had not uttered a single word despite hours of torture.

The soldiers forcefully inserted the funnel into the orc's mouth and poured a small bottle of truth serum. Only after waiting for him to swallow it whole did the general's men pull the funnel out of his mouth. 

The orc tried unsuccessfully to spit out what had been poured into him. His furious gaze slid quickly to the people around him, and his face showed deep surprise.  

He bellowed a few words in the ancient orcish tongue and then suddenly fell silent. His gaze clouded over, and his figure was slack as if it were not a living creature but merely a giant, clumsy puppet, the kind that often frightened children at the fair. The serum had begun to take effect.

One of the interrogators began to ask questions, and the scribe next to him began to write down everything the orc said. The orc's speech was rambling, and the language he spoke was one of the ancient dialects of the orcish language. However, the scribe quickly and skillfully wrote down all the words of the orc, so there was no doubt that not only goblins could boast of their knowledge of ancient languages.

The longer the orc answered the questions, the more anxious and worried the faces of all the interrogators and General Li, who also appeared to understand the orc's speech perfectly. 

What the orc had said didn't make sense. 

The interrogation ended, and the alchemist was asked to leave the tent. The general thanked the goblin and told him to keep a few more serum bottles for possible interrogation. He ordered the alchemist to be escorted to where the goblin squad awaited him. 

As he left the military camp, Galdan was excited and replayed everything he had heard today. However, the last part of the interrogation of the captured orc was the one that kept him most anxious...
