# Chapter Nine. The Price of Greatness.

"... In the Golden Age, the high priestesses of the cult of our mother Atonoel possessed the Talent of Clairvoyance. They protected the elven people from danger and guided them towards greatness and power. No other race could compare to us at that time.

But during the War with the Mad God, the high priestesses of the cult perished. The last of them died during the attack of the undead legions on the ancient capital of the elves, Larialionel, when the main temple of our mother Atonoel was destroyed. Only one of the young priestesses managed to survive.

My bloodline traces back to her. For millennia, the Talent of Clairvoyance was passed down through the women in our family.

It awakened in me when I was just a child. I remember my excitement and joy, realizing that I was the only one to inherit it in our family.

I believed that my gift could bring our people to greatness once more and that our power should extend far beyond the kingdom of Mitremar. Elves have been too afraid to venture beyond the borders of our forests for millennia. We completely shut ourselves off from the outside world and, like in a swamp, became mired in internal strife and intrigues. All our dreams of glory and greatness remained only in the dusty pages of ancient tomes.

How naive I was to think that my father would support my desires and aspirations. He didn't even want to hear about it. All he wanted was for me to fulfill my role as a priestess of the cult and use my gift to strengthen his rule.

How foolish and shortsighted he was! How could we, the elves, restore our former might if I waste my gift on the fleeting ambitions of my father? He does not deserve to be king for his people. The voices of our ancestors whispered to me that only I was destined to become a great ruler and lead the elves to greatness.

And so I remained silent when, in my visions, I saw that my father would break his neck during the royal horse races at the Summer Solstice festival of Mid with Noelle. A few days later, everything unfolded exactly as I had foreseen.

After the funeral, I was crowned, but due to my youth, my uncle was appointed regent until I came of age. But I couldn't wait another fifty years while foolish old men, led by my uncle, schemed behind my back.

Through a series of tragic accidents, all of my older relatives perished. I had foreseen all these events thanks to my gift. And though it was hard to lose my kin, it only strengthened my faith that Providence itself had chosen me for a great mission.

The year after my father's death, I, Merael Nyeven, finally became the Queen of the elves of Mitremar. Ahead of me lies much work on the path to my ultimate goal..."

"... It has only been a couple of hundred years, but I have already achieved much. The influence of the kingdom of Mitremar has spread far beyond its borders. The Kingdom of Aferia will, in a few generations, willingly become our vassal. The Balhian Khanate and the Emirates of Eastern Balhia, through my intrigues, are ready to begin war with each other once more. All it takes is a spark, and the conflict will ignite. And when both ruling dynasties weaken in this conflict, it will be time for my puppets to take the stage. They will dethrone their rulers. Even the unexpected invasion of orcs, as unlikely as it seems, may turn out to be beneficial to me. The Dujun Empire has been a serious obstacle on my path..."

"... Everything has gone wrong. How frustrating! Why didn’t the visions warn me that such evil would enter the world? Alright, Merael, get yourself together. Think about how to turn this situation to your advantage..."

"... Yes, that's right. The voices told me that I need to lead an alliance to fight the undead legions. The wise queen of the elves was able to unite all the peoples, and thanks to that, we achieved victory over the ancient evil. Ha-ha! Isn't this the perfect opportunity to show the strength and greatness of all the elven people..."

"Death and destruction for all. This is all I see in my visions. Why is this happening? My efforts and my entire people will be consumed by the fire that will devour this world. It was madness to believe we could defeat a God. Where are you, voices? Why are you silent and not speaking to me? Mother, where are you? I’m scared..."


## Storyboard for the Cinematic Trailer

**Scene 1**

**The shot reveals a stone hall with an altar positioned in its center. Resting upon the altar, secured within a specialized mount, is the Crown of the North. Several dwarves—rune-casters—stand around it, chanting incantations. Dwarven runes upon the pedestal and altar begin to ignite with a blue light. The intricate dwarven patterns on the Crown itself also begin to glow blue.**

**A dwarf clad in robes approaches the Crown and, using tongs, extracts the Adamantium crystal. The camera zooms in on the crystal until it fills the entire frame.**

*Voiceover: "Adamantium—the blood of Adomion. The source of boundless power, beyond even the wildest dreams of mortals."*

**Scene 2**

**A close-up of the Adamantium crystal. The camera slowly zooms out, revealing that the crystal is resting upon a velvet cushion inside a small chest. The camera pulls back further, showing the closed chest atop a pedestal inside a carriage. Six dwarven guards in full plate armor sit within the carriage as well.**

**As the camera continues to pull back, we see the carriage traveling along a road through a forest, escorted by riders and dwarven warriors on foot.**

*Voiceover: "Visions granted me this knowledge, and voices whispered how to use it to achieve my goal."*

**Scene 3**

**The forest road where the guarded convoy is moving. The camera rises higher, showing the treetops where elven archers lie in wait. At a certain moment, they begin to rain arrows down upon the convoy.**

*Voiceover: "Whoever wields the Adamantium may command legions of the undead. Its power is even enough to strip away the shackles of death. We can be immortal, just like the Tara themselves."*

**Scene 4**

**The aftermath of the defeated convoy. The carriage stands riddled with arrows, surrounded by the fallen bodies of humans and dwarven warriors. Nearby lies a dead human mage, his severed hand still clutching his staff.**

**Elves with bared swords stand near the carriage, with several captured warriors kneeling before them. Galador, the elven captain, emerges from the carriage holding the chest. He looks at the prisoners and gives a signal with his hand.**

**New shot: The elves bring their swords down upon the prisoners.**

*Voiceover: "But it will become useless if Adomion dies. Therefore, I cannot allow that to happen."*

**Scene 5**

**A forest glade where Queen Meraerl Nieven stands surrounded by her guard. Captain of the Guard Galador kneels before her, holding out the chest.**

**Meraerl removes the Adamantium from the chest.**

**A medium shot (waist-up): Meraerl holds the Adamantium and raises it above her head. As magical words escape her lips, the crystal begins to glow with a purple light. At that very moment, her appearance begins to transform.**

**The Queen's skin turns from light brown to a pale gray. Her hair shifts from raven-black to a deathly white, and dark shadows pool beneath her eyes. Her rosy lips turn black, and her beautiful green eyes change to purple.**

**Behind her, the exact same metamorphosis occurs simultaneously among her guards.**

**At the end of the scene, Meraerl sets the Adamantium into a pendant on a chain and hangs it around her neck. A satisfied, predatory smile plays across her face.**

*Voiceover: "With the Adamantium, I will bend Adomion's power to my will and become like a god. Моу dominion over the world shall be absolute."*
