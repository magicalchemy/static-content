# Chapter Seven. A New Alliance.

The ancient capital of the Erteland Empire, Minas, shimmered under the golden rays of the sun. A day so beautiful as if the world itself were not on the brink of annihilation. Galdan strolled through the city's streets, unhurried for once. It was rare for him to be alone; the Guardians never left his side.

The Child of Prophecy. The Chosen One. The alchemist who had become a living legend…Galdan let out a weary, bitter chuckle. So many titles.

The faces of the townsfolk were dark with worry and fear, a stark contrast to the illusion of peace cast by the sunlit skies.

Every day, hundreds of refugees arrived in Minas from nearby villages and settlements. Fleeing their homes, piling their belongings into wagons and carts, they sought safety behind the capital’s high walls, hoping to escape Adomion’s Curse.

But Galdan knew better than most: walls and soldiers meant nothing when faced with what was coming. The Dudzhun Empire had learned that the hard way.

He sat on a secluded bench in a park near a small pond, watching a plump, sleepy duck float lazily on the water.

It had been several days since he arrived in Minas alongside Gorl, his mentor and adoptive father, and the Goblin Elders, all to attend the Grand Council of Dji’Da’s greatest rulers.

The meeting was set to begin precisely at noon, and Galdan had chosen to spend his remaining time wandering the shaded avenues of the city.

"I didn’t expect the Chosen One of the goblins to favor solitude."

A soft, melodic female voice reached his ears, smooth as running water. Galdan turned and nearly gasped. Standing behind him was an elven woman clad in an ornate, flowing gown perhaps the most breathtakingly beautiful being he had ever seen.

Raven-black hair cascaded freely over her delicate shoulders, and her large emerald-green eyes regarded him with quiet curiosity.

"M-Merael Nyven? Lady Merael, Clear-Eyed Queen of the Wood Elves?"

"I am pleased to finally meet you, Galdan," she said, her lips curving into a small smile.

"Truthfully, I had hoped to do so half a year ago, when I first arrived in Echoron. Your kin impressed me their tales of the Great Prophecy, of their search for a new homeland. But most of all"

A single elegant brow arched ever so slightly.

"Their tales of you, the Child of the First Generation."

Galdan watched, transfixed, as Merael stepped to the water’s edge, arms crossed loosely over her chest. He knew full well that this council would not be happening without her.

The rulers of the other kingdoms had been all too eager to dismiss the horrors unfolding across the world and even more eager to ignore the Great Prophecy altogether. Only Merael Nyven had taken the Goblin Elders seriously.

"How fares the work of your alchemists?"

Her voice was like the song of a nightingale light, effortless, entrancing. Galdan cleared his throat.

"Ahem. Slow, Your Majesty. The alchemical labs in Echoron require an entire week to synthesize a single drachm of MANA, and we need no less than an ounce. It will take another month, but after that, we can begin the Rubedo process and synthesize PRANA."

"A month…"

Merael repeated the word slowly, as if tasting it.

"Whether we get that month depends on the Council’s decision. The enemy does not sleep, Chosen One."

She turned her gaze to him, and for the first time, he saw the steel behind her beauty.

"The other rulers still believe your people’s warnings to be myths bedtime stories meant to frighten children."

Her eyes narrowed.

"But I do not. I know the threat is real. And deadly. The fall of the Dujun Empire is proof enough of that."

A cool breeze stirred her hair as she spoke her next words.

"I will do everything in my power to protect my people. Even if this gathering of blind men chooses to ignore reason today, know this, Chosen One. I am your ally. If none of them extend their hand to you, you will always find mine waiting."

Galdan was caught off guard. Her words held weight. And more than that they felt sincere.

"Your words mean a great deal, Queen Merael," he said finally.

"There can be no greater friend to the goblins than you."

Merael smiled. A beautiful, unreadable smile sharp as a dagger’s edge.

"In times such as these, Chosen One…"

Her voice was quiet, yet undeniable.

"The most important thing is finding the right ally."

By the time Galdan reached the Royal Palace of Minas, noon had nearly claimed the sky. Straightening his cloak and ensuring his clothes looked at least somewhat dignified, he stepped onto the palace’s grand stone steps.

Now, standing at the threshold of Dji’Da’s most powerful rulers, he knew—there was no other choice but unity. No matter the cost. The only question was whether they could make the rulers of the world understand it in time.


## Storyboard for the Cinematic Trailer

**Scene 1**

**On board a boat stands a girl in period armor of medieval China or Japan, looking at the riverbank. Her head is bandaged, and only one eye is visible. In the distance, a panoramic view of the ruined capital, Tenchang, opens up, with smoke from fires rising above it. A crowd of zombies stands on the river piers. Several boats carrying the last survivors float along the river itself. At the end of the scene, the girl turns toward the camera. Her face reflects anger and fury at the destruction of the capital.**

*Voiceover: "Death and destruction have descended upon the lands of Dji`Da."*

**Scene 2**

**A desert. In the foreground stands a zombie camel driver in bedouin attire. One of his eyes has popped out of its socket, and jagged wounds are visible on his face.**

**Important note for the artist: the eyes of all undead glow purple in all scenes, even if they are empty eye sockets.**

**In the background lie the bodies of dead camels, with bundles of goods tied to their backs; other bundles are scattered haphazardly across the sand. Nearby stands a torn tent where merchants were waiting out the night until they were attacked and killed by a wandering skeleton of a nameless traveler who died in this desert long ago. Several zombie merchants also stand around the camel corpses.**

*Voiceover: "From the southern deserts to the northern forests—ill tidings arrive from everywhere."*

**Scene 3**

**The frame shows a forest. In the background, on a small hill, an elven woman stands at full height. She is frozen in a half-turn toward the camera with a drawn bow. Behind her back hides a frightened elven child. Extreme tension and a focused gaze are written on the elven woman's face. Opposite her, closer to us and with his back to the camera, stands an undead elf dressed as a forest scout (his dead nature must be emphasized by the skin color and wounds on his body). The figure of the undead is visible in the frame at three-quarters length (down to the knees). The corpse walks toward the elven woman and reaches his hands out to her; 3–4 arrow shafts are already sticking out of his body.**

*Voiceover: "Adomion's Curse has already sown its seeds of evil across all lands."*

**Scene 4**

**The frame shows a large mausoleum of fallen dwarves. Bas-reliefs with traditional dwarven patterns and images of warriors in armor are visible on the walls. The vault of the mausoleum is supported by massive pillars, with magical crystals placed in special holders illuminating the entire hall. In the background on the right, facing us, stand 4–5 dwarven warriors in full plate armor, holding shields out in front of them. In the hands of several warriors are axes, one holds a war hammer, and another holds a morningstar.**

**The dwarves stand in an open area near the entrance to the mausoleum. In the foreground are sarcophagi with their lids overturned. A half-decayed corpse of a dwarf in armor crawls out of one sarcophagus: bare ribs are visible, and the arm is a bone with a bracer hanging from it. Nearby, two dwarven skeletons in rusty armor have already risen (it must be shown that the armor literally hangs on the bones, while some elements—such as bracers or greaves—are missing). The weapons in the skeletons' hands are left to the artist's imagination (axes, swords). The corpses look at the living dwarves and begin to move toward them.**

*Voiceover: "Neither strong walls, nor enchanted forests, nor mighty mountains can save you."*

**Scene 5**

**A steppe. In the center of the frame, an orc warrior stands at full height (a classic, massive look in the style of Warcraft). Fresh cuts are visible on his body, with blood dripping from them, but despite the wounds, the orc firmly holds a large axe before him. Fury is frozen on his face, his mouth is open—a fierce orcish battle roar erupts from it.**

**The orc fights zombie warriors. These are the reanimated undead of fallen soldiers of the Dudzhun Empire, clad in medieval Japanese or Chinese armor. Their bodies are half-decayed, and they hold spears and swords in their hands. These soldiers died six months ago in this area while trying to stop the invasion of the Southern Tribes' orcs. The zombie soldier standing directly opposite the orc has his chest cavity half-cleaved from an axe blow, but he continues to stand on his feet and hold a sword. To the right of the orc on the ground lies a half-bisected zombie without a lower body—it crawls on its hands toward the orc. In the background, several more zombie soldiers of the Empire are seen approaching the battle site. The orc is surrounded on all sides.**

*Voiceover: "It is time to forget past grievances and cast aside all disagreements. Only then is there a chance to survive."*

**Scene 6**

**The throne hall of the Erteland capital, Minas. In the center of the hall stands a huge round table, around which eight empty chairs are arranged in a semicircle. **The second chair is occupied by the ruler of the Emirate of East Balkhia, Alged Nasir (attire styled after 16th-century Persia or India).**

**The fifth chair is occupied by the Lord-Pontiff of the Holy See, Pavelius. Spotless white robes, a sun symbol embroidered in gold on his chest, and a wide-brimmed hat on his head.**

**The sixth chair is occupied by the king of Erteland, Burstan Trevis. A man of 40–45 years old with a stocky build, wearing a richly embroidered tunic of green-brown color.**

At this moment, a white seagull (albatross) flies into the frame, lands next to Galdan, and instantly transforms into Larus. The video ends on this poignant note.**
