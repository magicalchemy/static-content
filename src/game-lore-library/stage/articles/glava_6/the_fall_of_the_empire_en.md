# CHAPTER SIX. THE FALL OF THE EMPIRE.

“Hey, watch this!”

Brugg, a young but battle-hardened Guardian, balanced his short sword by the pommel on his flat nose, shuffling his feet in an awkward attempt at a dance. Of all the Guardians, Brugg was the most carefree—almost reckless at times—but when danger struck, he became truly lethal.

His absurd little performance drew quiet laughter from the goblins gathered around the fire. The mission they were on was of the utmost importance and had lasted so long that even the strongest among them—both in body and spirit—were utterly exhausted. Even Pigma, the grim-faced leader of the Guardians, allowed himself a dry smirk, recognizing that his warriors needed a moment of levity.

Galdan chuckled as well, watching from where he sat on a gnarled tree root, wrapped in a warm blanket while studying a map. His company was not far from another ruined Temple of Mendalef, deep within the Great Swamps of Aza, a western province of the Dudzun Empire.

How many years had this maddening race lasted?

Five?
F
ive years, locked in a desperate struggle against fate itself, hoping to recreate the Elixir of Eternal Sleep, just as the alchemists of old once did to defeat Adomion.

A sharp pulse of pain shot through Galdan’s skull. Another headache. They had become his constant companions.

Evening was settling over the camp, the scent of cooking food wafting through the air. The Guardians laughed and jested, teasing one another even in the heart of darkness, far from home.

Galdan envied them.

For just one day, he wished he could be normal. Not the Chosen One, not Mendelef’s Blessed, not the Child of Prophecy, as people had taken to calling him.

Every time he uncovered another temple of the Father of All Goblins, his alchemical talent surged beyond its limits, the visions filling him with knowledge that no mortal should possess. He had long since surpassed his masters, outstripping even the greatest alchemists. He had become so different from others that, in the end, he was always alone.

Larus had told him this was his burden to bear. That he had no say in his fate. That the Prophecy had already decided everything for him.

Lost in thought, Galdan didn’t notice when sleep claimed him.

He soared like a snow-white gull over oceans and continents, unable to drink in enough of this world’s beauty, of the freedom of flight.

Below him, Dji’Da glittered with countless lights, kingdoms and wilderness alike aglow—like fireflies scattered across a summer field. And Galdan knew, without understanding how, that these lights were souls.

Elven souls shone like bright white flames.  
Dwarven souls burned orange, like embers struck from a forge.  
Orcish souls blazed in crimson.  
  Goblin souls flickered in hues of green and blue.  
    And the vast sea of golden lights—undoubtedly, the souls of mankind.

From the northern reaches of Farnax to the southern isles of Khela, the world was alight.

But then… something shifted.

A terrible sorrow gripped him, a cold dread coiling in his gut.
  
  Jarah.
  
  The southernmost continent was engulfed in impenetrable darkness a monstrous abyss, its tendrils slithering outward like the arms of some nightmarish kraken, reaching toward the trembling lights, snuffing them out.
  
  The darkness spread.
  
  Faster. Faster.  
  
Until nothing remained but a vast ocean of black void.
  
  His wings faltered. And he fell.
Fell helplessly, endlessly, into the abyss.  

Galdan woke up with a scream.
It wasn’t the first time he had seen this nightmare.
But each time, he awoke drenched in sweat, his heart hammering against his ribs.
  
  The Guardians fell silent, turning to look at him.
After years of traveling alongside the Chosen One, they had grown used to his nightmares. They watched over him in these moments, ensuring he never harmed himself.

His safety was their highest duty.
Pigma rose from his seat and approached.

“Are you alright?” 
Swallowing the lump in his throat, Galdan nodded.

“Get some rest. We have a long road ahead tomorrow,” Pigma said before returning to his warriors, who were preparing for their night patrol.

Galdan crawled back into his cramped tent, forcing himself to try and sleep.

But the feeling of impending doom would not let him go.
