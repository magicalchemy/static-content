# Chapter Eight. The Crown of the North

The North Reach. Ranges of towering, unyielding mountains, forever shrouded in snow and ice. Beneath the howling wind and the merciless lashing of a blizzard, a detachment made up of the strongest and most experienced warriors and mages—humans, dwarves, elves, and goblins—pressed forward along narrow trails. The journey was grueling; rocks and shards of ice broke free from underfoot time and again, plunging into the vast depths below, but this was the only path to the detachment’s goal.

"Maybe we should have gone through the lands of the Northern Horde orcs after all?" Galdan muttered, his teeth chattering from the cold so violently that he feared he might bite his own tongue off.

"That’s exactly what I think, Chosen One," a stocky dwarf grumbled in response, his magnificent beard dusted with frost. "My hands are itching to cleave a green-skin or two..."

"Out of the question," interrupted Meraerl Nieven, who had set out alongside Galdan, leading the forest elves. "We have no time for that. Every day of delay brings the doom of this world closer; that is why we chose this route."

The elven queen walked slightly ahead, conferring with her scouts. Unlike the goblins, humans, and dwarves, the elves did not sink into the snow but stepped upon its surface as if they were completely weightless. Galdan caught himself feeling a twinge of envy toward such a trait. Right now, at least, it would have come in incredibly handy.

The mountains of the North Reach had a foul reputation for their fickle and unpredictable nature, frequent avalanches, and ice storms. Yet, as the third day of their journey neared its end, everything had gone surprisingly smoothly and without incident. Galdan wanted to believe that fortune was smiling upon them, but a nasty worm of doubt writhed deep within his soul.

Suddenly, the elves ahead came to a halt. Meraerl froze, her gaze turning completely glassy, her hand within its fur glove clenching and unclenching.

"What’s going on over there?" the dwarf walking beside Galdan asked, spitting out the snow that kept driving into his mouth, nose, and ears.

"Stop!" the elven queen’s loud cry pierced the freezing air.

However, the dwarf kept marching forward, paying no heed to anything. The Chosen One managed to halt just a second before a massive, sharp icicle, breaking silently from somewhere above, flashed with a deadly point and pinned the dwarf to the earth.

The remainder of the journey passed in sullen silence. The dwarves could not even take the body of their kinsman with them to give him a proper burial. Closer to evening, the detachment found a more or less suitable spot to rest and pitched a meager camp. Conversation wouldn't flow, and glances darted anxiously upward far more often than they had just a day before.

After supper, Galdan did not head to his own tent but made his way toward Meraerl’s pavilion, where Galador—the captain of the royal guard, boundlessly loyal to his sovereign—stood watch. Before Galdan could utter a single word, Galador pulled back the tent flap, releasing a soft, warm light from within.

"You are expected, Chosen One."

The goblin cleared his throat, adjusted his cloak, and stepped inside the pavilion. The elf whose beauty was praised by every bard across Khela, deemed the fairest of women and the subject of countless romantic ballads, sat upon a folding camp stool holding some book. Even in such a simple setting, Meraerl looked graceful and majestic.

"Your Majesty..."

"Galdan, please, let us do away with these formalities. Call me by my name when we are alone."

The Chosen One nodded. He felt awkward every time he found himself one-on-one with her.

"Forgive me, Meraerl. It seems you saved my life today. The mountains would have taken me too, if not for your warning. How did you know what was about to happen?"

The elven queen's gaze grew pensive and sorrowful.

"I suppose I can tell you. You will understand me better than most others. I am also Chosen, in a way..."

Pausing for a moment, the queen continued:

"The Gift of foresight has been passed down through my bloodline for many thousands of years. Though, I cannot boast of its full power. I see only vague fragments and occasionally hear voices that warn of what may come to pass.

As sad as it is for me to say, with each generation, the Gift grows weaker. I know something similar happens among the goblins with your Talent for alchemy. After all, we are the children of our Tara.

And when they abandoned us, we began to lose their divine nature and blessing. Time will pass, and our Talents will vanish completely. Even magic itself will fade with time. All that made us grand will be gone. And even we, the elves, will become an ordinary, unexceptional people. Is this what our Parents desired for us?"

With those words, Queen Meraerl cut her speech short. Her eyes stared right through Galdan. A shiver ran down his spine at the thought that, perhaps at this very moment, the queen was gazing into that dark future. He certainly would not want to possess such a gift.

"However, I can aid you with more than just philosophical reflections," the elven queen smiled, and the pavilion seemed to brighten. "I am one of the most powerful sorceresses of this world, and I master portal magic. I knew I had to be a part of this vanguard the very moment we first met in Minas. Our destinies are bound, Chosen One. We are both fated to save our peoples."

Later, lying inside his fur sleeping bag and trying to bring some warmth back to his stiffened body, Galdan thought with relief that he had found in Meraerl not only a powerful ally, but someone who shared his anxieties and turmoil. She understood what it felt like to bear the crushing weight of an entire nation's fate upon one's shoulders.

## Storyboard for the Cinematic Trailer

**Scene 1**

**A top-down view: the map of Dji’Da. A dotted line traces the path from the capital city of Minas to the continent of Farnax, and further up north into the lands of the Northern Horde. Meanwhile, small inset frames showcasing fragments of this journey appear in the upper right and lower corners of the map screen (creating a route-tracking effect, similar to the Indiana Jones films):**

**A fragment showing ships tossing dynamically amidst a violent storm.**

**A fragment showing Grashir, the capital city of the Sandorn Kingdom.**

**A fragment where the ruins of an ancient temple of Mendelef peek through dense thickets.**

*Voiceover (Queen Meraerl Nieven): "Galdan led us to the north of the Farnax continent. It was here that we needed to find an ancient dwarven relic—the Crown of the North. The Adamantium adorning it was the sole purpose of our expedition."*

**Scene 2**

**A rocky cliff overlooking a panoramic view of the northern lands. Standing upon the summit alongside the Chosen One and several goblin guards are the elves, led by Queen Meraerl the Clear-Eyed in full battle armor, a pair of dwarves, a few human warriors, and a mage. Below, a view of a massive orc settlement situated in the valley opens up. The entire detachment stares intently down at it, as Galdan points his hand toward the camp.**

**Winter. A coniferous forest, heavy snow weighing down the branches of the trees. A close-up shot of the orc settlement: it is enclosed by a high wall of sharpened wooden stakes, and the dwellings are decorated with bones and skulls. Thick snow rests on the roofs of the buildings. Clear signs of a recent, brutal battle are visible everywhere: the gates are smashed open, and wide breaches yawn in the fortifications.**

*Voiceover: "For those crystals were the frozen droplets of Adomion's own blood. Without them, it was impossible to brew the Great Potion."*

**Scene 3**

**A view of the settlement's central square. Some of the houses are completely demolished, and traces of fires are visible—the village is utterly abandoned. The mauled corpses of orcs with severed limbs lie scattered across the streets.**

**Upon a raised dais in the square stands a chieftain's throne, adorned with the bones of humans and dwarves, its backrest crowned with shattered dwarven and human helmets and skulls. Seated upon the throne is the massive corpse of the orc chieftain. Resting on his head is the Crown of the North—a wide circlet with a massive red gemstone in the center, embellished with dwarven patterns and runic inscriptions. Deep, jagged wounds cover the chieftain's entire body.**

*Voiceover: "But we did not yet know that Adomion's Curse had already taken hold across these lands."*

**Scene 4**

**The throne is framed at a three-quarter angle; in the background, the ground is carpeted with the bodies of his fallen warriors.**

**The chieftain's head hangs lifelessly down and to the left. Standing with their backs to the camera before the throne are the figures of the elven queen, a human warrior in armor, two of the queen's personal elven guards, and our main hero, the goblin Galdan. The goblin slowly steps toward the corpse and reaches out his hand toward the Crown of the North.**

**At that exact moment, the eyes of the dead chieftain violently ignite with an otherworldly purple glow, and he snaps his head up. Simultaneously, in the background, the corpses of the other orcs begin to rise, their eye sockets flashing with the same purple fire.**

**Scene 5**

**An unreleased fire ball, conjured by the elven queen, slams into the rising corpse of the chieftain. The powerful blast knocks the Crown of the North off his head, and it drops directly to Galdan’s feet. Roaring flames completely engulf the chieftain's corpse as it collapses back onto the bone throne.**

**Scene 6**

**The camera begins to pan up from the burning throne. Part of the detachment comes into frame: Meraerl, throwing herself into a battle stance, and Galdan, who swiftly scoops the crown up from the ground.**

**The camera rises even higher, revealing the warriors of the detachment forming a tight, defensive ring around the throne. Standing in formation are 8–9 goblin guards, 6 human warriors, 6 dwarven warriors, and 6 elven guards, while the mages and Galdan occupy the absolute center of the circle.**

**The camera pulls out to a wide bird's-eye view from maximum height. It reveals that the detachment is completely surrounded on all sides by the encroaching undead orcs. The corpses crawl out of the ruined houses, flooding every alleyway and choking the settlement's streets; a few severed torsos drag themselves toward the ring of defenders on their bare hands. From the defenders' side, the first volleys of fiery orbs and the purple lightning of the mages unleash into the advancing mass of monsters. On this climactic note, the trailer abruptly cuts to black.**
