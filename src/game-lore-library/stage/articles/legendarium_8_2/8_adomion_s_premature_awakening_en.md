## Adomion's Premature Awakening

![](images/adomion_awakening.2x.png)
*Adomion, awakened before his time*

The ancient goblin prophecy did not promise victory. It offered only a warning: one day the Mad God would open his eyes again, and every living thing in Dji'Da would hear his breath. Since then, the alchemists of the Academy had not been searching for a weapon, a banishing spell, or a new way to destroy a deity. They were trying to restore the recipe of the Divine Elixir — the potion that had once forced Adomion into slumber while he still wore a physical form.

The work moved slowly. Old records contradicted each other, fragments of formulas were damaged, and some ingredients survived only in the retellings of those who had long since turned to dust. The alchemists argued, checked proportions, gathered samples, sent caravans to forgotten ruins, and returned to their cauldrons again and again. Each experiment gave them another reason to hope, but none became the recipe capable of holding a god's nature in chains.

Then Vitalius's formulas disappeared. At the Academy, some called it carelessness, while others suspected another desperate attempt to hasten the research. In the Fallen Moon tavern, as usual, witnesses immediately appeared and claimed to know everything. Someone swore that Biba and Boba had stolen the notes and were brewing some suspicious muck in the corner. Someone else insisted that their cauldron smelled as if hope, malt, and common sense had all died in it at once. Vitalius later said that rumors were not proof. But he said it in a way that suggested he was no longer fully convinced himself.

If that story truly had anything to do with Adomion's awakening, no one can prove it now. Perhaps the seals weakened on their own. Perhaps the ancient chains simply failed with time. Perhaps someone's foolishness gave the Mad God the push he needed before the appointed hour. For the survivors, the difference is small: the alchemists did not finish in time.

Adomion awoke not as a ruler returning to his throne, but as a being torn out of sleep by pain and rage. The remnants of the ancient elixir still burned in his divine blood. Broken chains hung from his four arms. Behind him rose the mutilated souls of those who had waited for his whisper from the depths for centuries. Wherever his will touched the land, roads emptied, caravans vanished, and shadows grew darker than they should.

The Academy fell first. Not because its walls were weak, but because it was the place where people had believed the longest that the recipe could still be found. Some halls were destroyed, some archives were lost, and many alchemists scattered across the roads of Dji'Da. The last messages from the Academy no longer read like scientific reports. They sound like short notes written by people trying to warn the rest of the world before silence reaches them too.

The Fallen Moon remained where it had always been: between the road, fear, and the stubborn habit of keeping the door open. Bugbear does not promise salvation or draw grand plans. He pours drinks for those who reach him alive and asks no unnecessary questions. In times like these, a tavern becomes more than a place to drink. It is shelter, rumor post, card table, bank of the last RUSK, and a strange point on the map where the world has not fully surrendered.

Silver has lost its old meaning. When markets fall silent, shops close, and distant locations empty out, wealth is measured differently. RUSK remains the last currency people still accept at the bar and at the card table. It is argued over, risked, and saved for worse days. Outside the tavern, it is still a market token: the price can be tracked on [Dexscreener](https://dexscreener.com/polygon/0x9ba3a271cce3e93c05d35e66b15d25ac51478206), and swaps can be made through [Uniswap](https://app.uniswap.org/swap?outputCurrency=0x231878d973C09fBCc0CA2239fFfb717795402cB4&chain=polygon). Respect is no longer counted in numbers: it can only be earned, lost, or heard in the brief dry words, "Respect +".

Those who left repairmen, alchemists, pets, wagons, or other valuables in old places should recover them while the passages remain open. Old vaults and custodial Dynamic wallets do not belong to the new order of the Fallen Moon. Anything that can be moved to a real Web3 wallet should be moved without delay. In a world where Adomion has awakened, postponing important business has become a particularly dangerous habit.

The last records of the surviving alchemists will appear in the [Academy channel](https://t.me/alchemania). The conversations of those still gathered by the fire continue in [@magicalchemychat](https://t.me/magicalchemychat) and [@magicalchemygamechat](https://t.me/magicalchemygamechat).

This is not the beginning of a grand campaign and not a promise of swift victory. It is the chronicle of a plan that did not become salvation in time. But as long as people still play cards in the Fallen Moon, argue over RUSK, repeat rumors about Vitalius, Biba, and Boba, and keep contact with the last surviving alchemists, the story of Dji'Da is not over.

Adomion has awakened. The Academy lies in ruin. The world has become smaller, darker, and quieter. But the tavern light still burns.
