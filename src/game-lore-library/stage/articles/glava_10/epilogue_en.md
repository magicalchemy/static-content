# Storyboard for the Final Cinematic (Epilogue)

**Scene 1**

The frame shows Gorl's workshop study. Large bookshelves line the walls, packed with various objects: alchemical tools, ancient manuscripts, and artifacts. Near the window stands a large writing desk with carved legs, flanked by a massive leather armchair.

New shot: Gorl sits at the writing desk (angled in a three-quarter view), writing with a quill on the page of a thick tome. He finishes his entry, sets the quill down on the desk, and closes the heavy book.

Camera change (POV shot from over Gorl's shoulder): He gently, barely touching the surface, wipes away non-existent dust from the leather binding. A close-up reveals the title on the cover: “The Chronicles of Ekhoron. The Life and Deeds of Gorl DaNarg and His Glorious Son, Galdan DaGorl.”

Camera change: Gorl exits the study, carefully carrying a beautifully decorated chest under his arm.

**Scene 2**

The frame opens on a clear, bright summer day. The stately figure of Gorl, wrapped in a travel cloak, walks down the wide, clean streets of Ekhoron. Flawless cobblestones crunch beneath his feet, and a fountain flows steadily on a street corner. Neatly dressed goblins populate the streets; some chat peacefully, while others go about their business, nodding respectfully to Gorl as he passes by.

Camera change: Gorl arrives at a vast, blooming park. Green lawns stretch across the area, decorative trees grow tall, and lush flowerbeds line the pathways. Children from different nations—goblins, humans, and elves—laugh and run past the old goblin, playing together.

Joining the main avenue, Gorl merges into a dense, peaceful stream of goblins, humans, dwarves, and orcs walking forward.

**Scene 3**

A panoramic view of a wide plaza in the absolute center of the park, where radial avenues converge. In the middle of the plaza stands a monumental, 15-meter-tall statue of Galdan crafted from pure white marble. The Chosen One stands upon a high pedestal, at the base of which are carved bas-reliefs representing the four mortal races: a human mage, a dwarven warrior, an elven archer, and an orc with an axe. Before the statue is a massive memorial, completely covered in flowers and burning lamps. At the base stands a guard of honor composed of goblin sentries in full dress armor.

Camera change: A view from behind Gorl's back. He steps at full height toward the memorial, which is buried under fresh flowers and candles lit inside lamps shaped like alchemical flasks. Next to him, a young goblin alchemist carefully places his own flask-lamp, while slightly further away, a beautiful elven woman in a lavender dress lowers a large basket of fresh flowers to the ground. In the background, hundreds of figures stand—goblins, humans, dwarves, and orcs. The entire world has come to show respect and gratitude for the sacrifice of Galdan.

Gorl pulls out the chest, opens it, and gently sets it down on an empty space free of flowers. For a brief second, he rests his palm on the pedestal. He stands for a moment, gazing up at the marble face of his adopted son, then smiles slightly, gives a quiet nod of his head, turns, and walks away.

Camera change: A close-up shot of the open chest. Resting smoothly inside on a velvet cushion are the four elemental runes from the rune quests. A gentle summer wind begins to blow, carrying flower petals across the frame. The camera catches this movement, tracking the pirouettes of the petals through the air. The background softly blurs, and the camera accelerates upward, taking a bird's-eye view to reveal a stunning panorama of the revived city: majestic alchemist towers, bustling marketplaces, and caravans of wagons. Everything gleams under the bright sunbeams.

The camera rises even higher toward the roofs of the buildings, stopping on the ridge of a roof where a single white seagull sits. The bird watches the plaza and the monument of Galdan intently. A moment later, it lets out a piercing cry, spreads its powerful wings, and takes flight from the rooftop ridge.

The frame sharply cuts to black. The credits begin.

The first final cinematic is fully complete.
