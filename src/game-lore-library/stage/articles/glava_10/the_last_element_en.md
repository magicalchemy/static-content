# Chapter Ten. The Final Element.

The cold wind from the sea tore at his cloak, soaked with salt and moisture. Waves crashed against the wooden pilings of the pier with a dull roar, spraying everything around with fine water mist. Against the backdrop of the early dawn, where the sky shifted from pale gray to steel, the silhouette of the ship stood out as a dark mass.

The caravel, provided to them by the Grand Duchy of Rado, was stunning in its grandeur: pure white sails, a sturdy hull made of dark oak, and sparkling metallic decorations on the bow. It stood out from the other merchant and fishing ships in the harbor, like an alien from another world in this dreary place.

Galdan stood at the edge of the pier, gripping the wooden railing so tightly that his fingers turned white. His gaze was fixed on the ship, but his thoughts were far away.

Months of travel had brought him here, to the port of Frow, following visions that had plagued his nights. He had seen her in these visions, like a flash of light against the darkness. Merael Nyeven, holding the gleaming Adamantium in her hands. Her eyes burning with violet light, her silver hair flowing as if alive. She was no longer the person he had known.

The queen of the forest elves, Merael Nyeven, once trusted, had betrayed them all. She had destroyed everything they had built and disappeared, taking with her the cursed relic. The Adamantium turned out to be something entirely different. It was drops of the blood of the Mad God Adomion, frozen and crystallized.

This blood had been the last and most crucial ingredient for the Great Dream Elixir. The power within the Adamantium could change the world. The fact that Merael could now wield this power for evil filled Galdan with cold dread.

Each vision brought Galdan closer to her. The visions told him that the elven queen had gone to an island, the one called the Twilight Wastes. A place even the legends deemed cursed. No one ever returned from there. But he had to go after her. The fate of all the living in the world rested on his mission. That’s why he was here, at the last stage of the journey.

"New, shiny, and inside, it'll probably rot in three days," muttered Pigma, the old goblin guard, looking at the ship.

He stood a little to the side, leaning on his massive iron halberd. His leather armor was covered in travel dirt, just like Pigma himself—short, stocky, with grayish skin and warts that added to his gruffness. His dark eyes gleamed like embers, despite his age.

Galdan gave a barely noticeable nod but didn’t respond.

"The Chosen One, you’re thinking about her again," Pigma said, resting on his halberd.

"You always say that," Galdan replied.

"Because you’re always thinking about her," the guard retorted. "And that’s the whole cycle."

Galdan sighed heavily.

"She was my friend, Pigma. How could I not trust her? How could I not see that she wanted to betray us?"

"Enough!" the old guard barked. "You didn’t see it because no one would have seen it. That cursed elf tricked us all. Your mistake is that you still see her as the person she was. Forget it. The Merael who sat with us by the fire, drinking from the same mug, is gone. The one who was on our side died the day she took the Adamantium."

Galdan closed his eyes. The moments when he trusted her came back to him. Merael Nyeven had always been his embodiment of wisdom and strength. Together they devised plans, created alchemical potions, and talked about uniting all races for a common goal. She had been a symbol of hope.

He remembered her smile, warm, reassuring. And that cold gaze, like polar ice, that she cast upon him in his visions. Holding the cursed artifact.

"She knew what she was doing," Pigma continued, his voice sharpening. "And she chose it."

"But why?" Galdan suddenly asked, turning to the guard. "Why? We had a plan. We could have made it. Together."

"Because of power. Because of ambition. Because everyone who touches the artifacts of the gods thinks they can control them."

The old goblin fell silent for a moment before adding:

"And also because she was smart enough to know we couldn’t stop her."

"The Chosen One, you understand that she won’t stop," Pigma continued. His voice softened, but it still carried a harsh edge. "If she reaches the Twilight Wastes and frees Adomion... it’s the end for all."

Galdan turned away. Everything Pigma said was true. But that didn’t lessen the pain. He stood, looking at the horizon where thick clouds were beginning to gather, as if warning of an approaching storm.

Darkness awaited them ahead, in the Twilight Wastes. But it didn’t matter. Because Merael was there. And he knew his mission wouldn’t end until they met again.

## Storyboard for the Final Cinematic

**Scene 1: The Twilight Wastes**

The barren lands of the Twilight Wastes. The island earned its name because the sky above is in perpetual semi-darkness, choked by ash and smoke from hundreds of erupting volcanoes. It is a lifeless territory, lashed by constant storms and lightning, with air poisoned by sulfur and toxic gases.

View from a high hill. Panorama: A solitary mountain stands before us, its sharp peaks piercing upward. On the mountain slope, excavations of the entrance to Adomion's Tomb are underway. Giant black gates are visible, covered in bas-reliefs depicting unknown creatures and monsters. At the foot of the mountain, before the gates, lies the camp of Queen Meraerl’s dark elves and the army of the undead that unearthed the tomb's entrance.

**Scene 2: The Hill Near Adomion's Tomb**

Close-up: Queen Meraerl stands at full height on the hill, preparing to perform a magic ritual to open the gates of the Mad God Adomion's tomb. She is fully focused on the ritual preparations.

Medium shot (waist-up): Suddenly, she turns sharply and peers into the distance with suspicion. A moment later, a self-satisfied smile appears on her face. She waves her hand, and the undead army begins to move, slowly advancing toward the distant hills.

**Scene 3: The Distant Hills**

Long shot. We see a ridge of hills. The camera zooms in on one specific hill. Galdan’s detachment emerges onto the ridge. In the foreground is Galdan himself, several Goblin Guardians in leather armor, a tall human warrior in plate armor, several dwarves in expensive dwarven armor, and four human Archmages: two men of European appearance (one elderly, the other in his forties), a third—a gray-haired Arab in traditional attire, and an elven woman. Behind this group, fifty to sixty warriors and mages are visible on the slopes.

The camera switches to a Point-of-View (POV) shot from the detachment. They see the lonely mountain and the wave of undead approaching them in the distance.

**Scene 4: The Distant Hills (The New Alliance)**

Close-up of the lead Archmage, the elderly man. He is the Master of the Bisaria Magic Academy. He gazes into the distance and nods to his comrades.

The camera pans, revealing the Archmages standing in a line, ten meters apart. They begin to cast, raising their staves.

The camera rises higher to show all four Archmages. Suddenly, magic portals open behind them (similar to the film Avengers: Endgame), and armies begin to emerge. From the first portal comes a dwarven hird in full plate armor and elves from the Kingdom of Adriennel.

The second portal: A human army. The third portal: An army of orcs on boar-rhinos and infantry units. The fourth portal: Another human army (depicting different armor styles from the first army).

**Scene 5: The Battlefield**

Clouds; a seagull flies across the frame. It soars over the battlefield. The camera glides over the positions. The Alliance of Mortals organizes its battle formations: dwarves with shields in front, humans with pikes beside them. Elves take the second rows, and orcs take the flanks. In the rear, dwarves load battle catapults with jars of alchemical fire mixture.

Everyone awaits the undead army—the corpses of human soldiers, commoners, dwarves, and orcs. As the wave of undead reaches the archers' line of fire, the elves and humans release arrows tipped with alchemical glass flasks. Upon impact, they instantly incinerate the undead into ash. Simultaneously, the catapults launch their projectiles.

**Scene 6: The Battle**

Aerial view. The undead army crashes into the first line of dwarves and humans. Close-quarters combat begins. A dynamic montage follows, with various moments of the great battle serving as the background:

A dwarf hurriedly cleaves a dead human warrior in grimy armor in half with an axe; in the background, dwarves have formed a shield wall.

An orc on a boar-rhino charges into the undead ranks, scattering the dead in all directions.

A square of pikemen (mercenaries from the Invia Union, depicted as Swiss-style pikemen) meets the undead wave with leveled spears. A dozen corpses are already impaled on the pikes, still ferociously reaching forward.

An elven guardsman with a glaive—its blade glowing with magic runes—decapitates the undead with a precise strike, causing them to crumble into dust.

**Scene 7: The Distant Hill, New Alliance Headquarters**

A shot from the hill where the Archmages stand at full height with their backs to the camera. We also see several hundred rank-and-file mages standing a short distance behind them. All are gazing intensely at the battlefield.

The lead Archmage raises his staff, which begins to glow brightly. Following his lead, all the mages raise hundreds of staves and magic wands.

New shot. A panorama of the battle; we see the countless hordes of undead. Suddenly, lightning strikes down from the sky, scattering the dead with every hit. Giant and small fireballs (depending on the caster’s strength) fly into their ranks. The spheres instantly incinerate vast numbers of undead. The ground begins to collapse beneath the army of the dead, and jets of molten lava erupt from beneath the earth, creating a massive flow that blocks the path of the undead to the New Alliance positions.

**Scene 8: The Hill Near Adomion's Tomb**

Close-up of Queen Meraerl. Her face is twisted in savage rage because she failed to destroy Galdan quickly. She barks out commands. Behind her, her personal guard elves immediately flank her. The Queen holds a pendant containing the Adamantium in one hand, while the other makes rapid magical passes. The pendant glows ominously purple. Suddenly, a black pentagram appears under the feet of the Queen and her guards, and they vanish in a bright flash.

**Scene 9: New Alliance Headquarters**

The hill where Galdan stands, surrounded by armed Guardians, dwarves, humans, and Archmages. A dark flash occurs behind the group, and Meraerl and her dark elves suddenly appear in the empty space.

None of the guards have time to react.

Meraerl unleashes black lightning from her hands, striking the four Archmages. They explode with devastating force. Her elven warriors begin a ferocious assault on the remaining mages and Galdan’s guards. Black lightning strikes the human mages, who begin to fall.

Medium shot (full height). We see Galdan surrounded by his guards. Suddenly, a massive explosion occurs nearby, throwing Galdan aside. He hits the ground hard, disoriented. Meanwhile, his entire guard is locked in a life-or-death struggle with Meraerl’s guards. No one is left to protect Galdan. Queen Meraerl slowly approaches him with a bared sword.

Close-up of the Queen. She offers Galdan the chance to reconsider and join her. He firmly refuses. She raises her sword for a killing blow.

Suddenly, a seagull drops from the sky onto the Queen's face, beating her ferociously with its beak and wings. Meraerl screams and lashes out with her sword.

Separate fast cut: During the distraction, Galdan regains his senses, pulls out his alchemical dagger, and drives it into Meraerl’s stomach with all his might.

Close-up (waist-up): Extreme surprise freezes on Meraerl’s face as she slowly looks down at her wound. She tries to speak, but only bloody bubbles escape her mouth. The Queen sways and falls to the ground.

New shot: Galdan looks at the dead Queen, approaches, and with a swift motion, rips the Adamantium pendant from her neck. He looks around in shock and sees Larus lying nearby. Larus is bleeding heavily, clutching a deep wound in his side—Meraerl had managed to strike him.

Galdan rushes to him and tries to help him sit up. Larus, weakening, says they must hurry, for Adomion will soon fully awaken and enter this world. Larus mutters something quickly, and Galdan uses the dagger he just used on Meraerl to make an incision on his own palm. Blood from the wound drips directly onto the Adamantium. The crystal ignites with a brilliant golden light and suddenly disintegrates into a fine, shimmering dust.

Galdan pulls the flask of the Great Sleep Potion from his tunic and carefully pours the Adamantium grains into it. The contents of the flask glow a pure gold, briefly illuminating the surroundings before fading. Galdan secures the flask back inside his tunic.

Close-up (waist-up) of Larus and Galdan. Larus says something important to Galdan, who responds calmly and quietly. Larus lowers his eyes, and a few tears slowly roll from beneath his eyelids. Galdan calmly pats his mentor on the shoulder and takes Larus firmly by the hand. Larus looks up at Galdan, nods silently, and with the last of his strength, squeezes Galdan’s hand. A bright flash. Galdan instantly vanishes, and Larus lets out a final breath, his head dropping to his chest. He dies.

**Scene 10: Entrance to Adomion's Tomb**

The shot shows the tomb entrance. Giant black gates loom before the camera. A flash occurs, and Galdan appears. He resolutely places his hand against the gate wing; a primal roar sounds, and the gates slowly part. Beyond the gates lies impenetrable, absolute darkness.

Galdan pulls the flask from his tunic. It glows with a faint, warm yellow light. Galdan turns back, taking one last look at the entire battlefield, then looks up briefly at the somber sky. He blinks slowly, saying goodbye to this world, turns back to the dark passage, and takes a resolute step into the darkness.

**Scene 11: The Distant Hill**

A distant view of the mountain containing Adomion's Tomb. Suddenly, a colossal explosion occurs at the very peak of the mountain. A powerful shockwave, rocks, and clouds of dust roar across the entire plain. The Alliance armies and the undead are completely engulfed in a massive cloud of dust.

From the very summit, a giant pillar of golden light erupts into the heavens. The mountain slope containing the gates collapses inward. The mountain peak also folds downward, burying the tomb. High in the sky, the pillar of light begins to spread outward in a powerful concentric wave toward every horizon.

Camera change. Medium shot: Human and dwarven warriors are seen along with the undead that were attacking them. Suddenly, all the undead fall to the ground at once, turning into motionless corpses. The warriors look around in shock, not understanding what is happening. Then they begin to triumphantly raise their weapons and scream with joy that they have won; soldiers embrace, cry, and help each other up from the ground. The scene fades. The screen turns completely black.

**Scene 12**

The scene begins with a black screen. Then, the darkness in the center parts upward and downward, then closes again. The effect mimics the POV of a person who has just regained consciousness, opened their eyes, and is blinking slowly.

Camera change. Before us is the silence of space and shimmering stars in the distance. Below, the beautiful blue planet—Dji’Da—is visible. Suspended in weightlessness against the planet's backdrop is Galdan. He is dressed in the same traveling clothes he wore when entering the Mad God's tomb.

A shot from behind Galdan's back. Galdan clearly sees a wide wave of golden radiance spreading in a perfect circle through the planet's atmosphere, originating from the location of the cursed Twilight Wastes. This wave gently traverses the entire horizon visible to Galdan, cleansing the world.

Galdan hears a beautiful, crystal-clear chime and slowly turns his head from the planet toward the source of the sound.

Camera change. Behind Galdan, in the vacuum of space, a wide band of dazzling light begins to expand. The colors in this radiant area slowly shift from pure white and pale yellow/green tones to a soft blue and pink (Visual reference: the finale of the film Ghost).

A shot at a distance from Galdan, waist-up from behind. In this soft white light, familiar images and silhouettes of various goblins, elves, humans, and other races begin to appear one by one. And closest to the boundary of light and space, the figure of Larus becomes clearly visible. There is no pain on his face; he reaches out a hand to Galdan, inviting his student to finally join them.

Galdan turns back, takes one last parting look at the saved planet Dji’Da, and confidently steps forward into the light. A happy, peaceful smile plays on his face. Larus smiles joyfully back at him. As Galdan approaches his mentor, the souls of the goblin alchemists approach him. They sincerely applaud Galdan, greeting him warmly, while someone pats him on the back like a father. This entire united group of figures slowly and beautifully dissolves into the pure white radiance.

At this moment, the scene cuts sharply to a black screen. Credits begin.

The first final cinematic is complete.
